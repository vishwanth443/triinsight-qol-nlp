# QoL Domains for AWH Patients

## Symptoms
Short definition: How the hernia’s physical effects (pain, movement restriction) limit daily activities and require adaptations.[file:4]

Key aspects:
- Pain level and pattern (constant, severe, night pain).[file:4]
- Freedom of movement (walking, stairs, bending, personal care).[file:4]
- Restrictions and adaptations (changing how they dress, shower, lift, play with children).[file:4]

Example phrases:
- “I have to move carefully and can’t do things the way I used to.”
- “The pain and discomfort limit my walking and daily tasks.”

## Body image
Short definition: How patients think and feel about the appearance of their abdomen and body, and the extent to which they feel abnormal, ugly, or ashamed.[file:2][file:4]

Key aspects:
- Negative self‑perception (feeling “out of shape”, “pregnant”, or “like a monster”).[file:2][file:4]
- Heightened self‑consciousness and shame in public.[file:2]
- Concealment strategies (baggy clothes, corsets, avoiding mirrors).[file:2][file:4]

Example phrases:
- “I feel ugly and try to hide my stomach all the time.”
- “I avoid tight clothes because I don’t want the hernia to show.”

## Mental health
Short definition: Emotional and psychological impact of living with the hernia, including low mood, anxiety, distress, and coping strategies.[file:4][file:1]

Key aspects:
- Emotional responses (sadness, depression, anxiety, frustration).[file:4]
- Feeling trapped, loss of control, or loss of previous identity (e.g. athlete, worker).[file:4]
- Coping strategies (humour, spirituality, mindfulness, routines, support).[file:4][file:2]

Example phrases:
- “I feel low and sometimes cry because of how my body is now.”
- “It feels like being stuck in a prison cell.”

## Interpersonal relationships
Short definition: Impact on social life, social confidence, and intimate/sexual relationships with partners, family, and friends.[file:4][file:2]

Key aspects:
- Social withdrawal or loneliness, avoiding going out or being seen.[file:4][file:2]
- Awkward interactions and fear of others’ reactions (staring, comments, questions).[file:2][file:4]
- Changes in sexual relationships (reduced intimacy, shame, fear of rejection).[file:2][file:4]

Example phrases:
- “I don’t go out much anymore because I’m embarrassed.”
- “Our sex life has dropped because I don’t want my partner to see my stomach.”

## Employment
Short definition: Effects on ability to work, job tasks, financial security, and the role as a provider.[file:4][file:3]

Key aspects:
- Difficulty performing physical work tasks or having to change duties.[file:4]
- Time off work, job loss, or early retirement due to hernia.[file:4][file:3]
- Financial pressure and perceived costs to family from reduced income.[file:4]

Example phrases:
- “I had to stop working because of the hernia and money is tight.”
- “If I don’t push through the pain, I can’t earn enough.”
